Active Culture

Food is at the intersection of science, art, culture, and politics. It is the connective tissue between otherwise disparate cultures, and a medium to share what makes those cultures unique. How are we feeding ourselves? How does food characterize our relationships with people, our bodies, and our environment? These are the questions that are asked by Active Culture, a student publication intent on cataloguing the present-day experience through the food that fuels it in the form of recipes, stories, academic writing, and everything in between.
